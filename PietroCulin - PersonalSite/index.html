Gotham, a font Study

For this font study project I decided to use black and white as main colours to point up it’s will to be a simple and geometric typeface. The decision to assign the colour cyan to the font was more for aesthetic reasons, through the idea was to make an indirect confrontation of the font with 2 different yet similar sans-serif typefaces, Futura and Avenir which have been identified respectively with the colours yellow and magenta in order to point up a sort of bond between the three fonts. This small expedient was made to emphasize the differences between those three fonts and to better appreciate the uniqueness of the “Gotham” typeface, it’s rules and it’s shapes.

Personal Monogram

The idea behind the monogram was to tell my style and personality. Four line segments, two orizzontals and two at 45° form two identical yet opposite figures for two different characters. It’s simple, linear and simmetrical, characteristics that always accompany my works.

Inspiration

Being a dreamer, I have always loved fantasy and hystorical genre. With that in mind I took inspiration from the cheltic alphabet to start shaping my monogram which, initially, was in fact made by two cheltic runes joined together although, truth be told, they didn’t refered to P and C (they looked similar though). Alteration were made to remove superfluous elements in order to make the monogram more simple and simmetrical.

Poster

Movie poster, made with only two colours (blak and red), dedicated to the horror film “Slender Man”, released in 2018 and directed by Sylvan White. The film itself wasn’t a big success and that reinforced my decision to make the drawing look like a children’s doodle. This wanted to be a reference to the homonym game “Slender man” from which the creepypasta got all its fame and recognition.

Postcard

A simple project, a little postcard, an invitation to a city chess tournament. The challenge of the project was given by the limitation of the print to only white printing on black cardboard. The idea of using chess as invitation theme made this single limitation the strong point of the project. The title, written with the font “Bleeding Cowboys”, is eyecatching and it’s design, not in line with a game as composed as chess evokes curiosity on the viewers.

A Loooong Night

“Characters in loop” is a simple yet funny challenge proposed at the end of the university course of Image and Sound, consisting of the animation of 5-15 sec. of a famous character of either a film or a Tv serie, the whole animation had to be put in a smooth and functional loop.

Concept Video

The video, dedicated to the famous TV series “Mr. Robot”, has been designed in an extremely simple style inspired by Mark Rise illustrations in order to make it easier to animate. The animation shows the protagonist of the series Elliot Alderson (Rami Said Malek) during is night activities as a haker, aside from him the smoke of the coffee and the clock are the only other animated object. The clock moves purposely faster than normal in order, with a deep, heavy and sinister song in background, to create an atmosphere of tension and distress to better describe the scene.
